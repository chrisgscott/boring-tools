Why "boring"?

Most productivity apps want your email, your credit card, and a monthly subscription. They need servers, databases, and analytics pipelines. They track what you do, when you do it, and how often you come back.

These tools don't do any of that. Each one is a single HTML file that runs in your browser. Your data lives in localStorage on your device. Nothing is sent anywhere. There are no accounts to create, no passwords to remember, no subscriptions to cancel.

That's boring. On purpose.

"Boring" means the tool does what it says and nothing else. No growth hacks, no engagement loops, no dark patterns. Just a thing that solves a problem and gets out of your way.

📁

Single file

Each tool is one HTML file. No build step, no node_modules, no deploy pipeline. Right-click, save, open in a browser.

🔒

Your data, your device

Everything stays in localStorage. No cloud sync, no analytics, no third-party tracking. Close the tab, your data is still there when you come back.

Desktop, tablet, phone. Any modern browser. Some tools support PWA install so you can add them to your home screen and use them offline.

Common Questions

What happens if I clear my browser data?

Your data will be gone. Each tool has a JSON export/backup feature. Use it periodically if your data matters to you.

Can I use these on my phone?

Yes. Open the tool in your phone's browser. All tools support "Add to Home Screen" for an app-like experience with offline caching. Every tool is responsive.

Will there be a paid version?

These tools are free. If premium features appear in the future (cloud sync, notifications, PDF reports), the core tools will stay free.

Can I self-host these?

Absolutely. Each tool is a static file. Drop it on any web server, CDN, or just open it from your filesystem. No server-side requirements.

How do I move my data between devices?

Export your data as JSON from one device, import it on the other. Manual, but deliberate. No cloud sync means no cloud risk.
